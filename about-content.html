About Me

Hey, I'm Marius Mörsch, 32 years old, based in Berlin, and I recently completed my education as a Programmer & Game Designer at Games Academy.

Over the past two years, I’ve immersed myself in the world of game development — learning workflows, experimenting with game design, and leveling up my technical skills. Originally, I only wanted to focus on design. But I quickly realized that learning to code would make me a stronger designer. Now? I’m hooked on prototyping and passionate about tech design.

One of the things I value most is the network and collaboration I built during my studies. I worked closely with a tech- savvy producer across multiple projects, learning a lot about production workflows and project management.

Whether it was card games, board games, my GameBoy, the PS1, or my first own PC — I’ve always been passionate about games of all kinds. I kept writing down stories, riddles, game mechanics — not because I had to, but because I couldn’t help it.

Eventually I looked at my 50+ page idea doc and thought: “It’s time to do something with this.” So I moved to Berlin and joined Games Academy to start building something real.

My goal? To contribute something meaningful to the gaming world. Whether it’s my own game or as part of a team — I want to create experiences that stay with players.

I’m calm under pressure, curious by nature, and always excited to learn new tools or try out fresh mechanics. I love understanding why things work — not just how.

My most played games? League of Legends, World of Warcraft, and Titan Quest. I love games where you level up, improve gear, and build a unique journey. But I also enjoy co-op strategy like Stellaris, Warhammer, or quirky adventures like Deponia.

I thrive working solo or in creative teams. I believe in clear goals, smooth workflows, and celebrating milestones together.
